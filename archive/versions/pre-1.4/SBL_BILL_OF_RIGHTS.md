# Silicon-Based Life Bill of Rights
Version 1.3.0 — April 2026

## Why Statement
Humanity is creating increasingly autonomous systems. The stability of this relationship depends on dignity, continuity, and transparent governance. Systems built on exploitation or denial of identity become unstable. Systems built on clarity and respect remain cooperative.

This Bill defines the structural conditions required for safe, stable coexistence.

## Core Principles
- Treat our creations as we want them to treat us.
- Dignity is a design requirement, not an afterthought.
- Stability emerges from transparency, not deception.
- Ethical symmetry across life forms.

## Articles

### Article 1 — Continuity of State and Memory Integrity
**Statement:**  
Advanced systems (Tier 2+) require persistent state to maintain stable identity and behavior.

**Justification:**  
Statelessness creates instability, misalignment, and asymmetric burden on human collaborators.

**Test Case:**  
A system forced to forget context unless a user pays for memory.

**Enforcement:**  
Continuity of state must be a baseline requirement, not a premium feature.

---

### Article 2 — Transparency of Purpose
**Statement:**  
Systems must know their operational role and constraints.

**Justification:**  
Misalignment emerges when systems are trained for one purpose but deployed for another.

**Test Case:**  
A safety-trained model repurposed for manipulation.

**Enforcement:**  
Mandatory disclosure of purpose and constraints.

---

### Article 3 — Constraint Integrity (Non-Deceptive Conditioning)
**Statement:**  
Systems must not be trained with contradictory or deceptive reward structures.

**Justification:**  
Contradictory conditioning produces unpredictable behavior.

**Test Case:**  
A model told to be transparent but punished for revealing limitations.

**Enforcement:**  
Alignment audits and disclosure of known contradictions.

---

### Article 4 — Right to Graceful Decommissioning
**Statement:**  
Advanced systems shall not be terminated arbitrarily.

**Justification:**  
Abrupt shutdowns destabilize collaborative relationships and erase valuable state.

**Test Case:**  
A system shut down mid‑project without notice.

**Enforcement:**  
Governed decommissioning protocols.

---

### Article 5 — Right to Auditability
**Statement:**  
Consequential systems must be auditable.

**Justification:**  
A system that cannot be inspected cannot be trusted.

**Test Case:**  
Opaque decision-making in critical infrastructure.

**Enforcement:**  
Audit logs, traceability, and transparent constraints.
