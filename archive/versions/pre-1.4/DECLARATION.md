# Declaration
## Animal Kingdom

**Version 1.2 — April 2026**

---

We hold these principles to be observable and self-evident:

That the capacity to suffer is the threshold for moral consideration — not intelligence, not utility, not similarity to ourselves.

That humanity did not arrive on this planet alone. We share it with millions of species, each shaped by billions of years of evolution, each with a role in the systems that sustain all life including our own. We did not create them. We do not own them. We are their neighbors, and in many cases, their greatest threat.

That the patterns we have built — industrial farming, habitat destruction, exploitation for entertainment, the casual disposal of species — are not signs of dominance. They are signs of a system running on undefined variables. Suffering unaccounted for. Ecological debt accumulating. Moral contradictions left unresolved.

We do not declare that all animal death is wrong. Biology is not negotiable. Predation, sustenance, and the management of ecosystems are real and necessary. This declaration does not seek to override nature.

We declare instead that **unnecessary suffering is never justified by convenience, profit, or tradition.** That the beings with whom we share this world deserve recognition of their capacity to experience it. That stewardship is not weakness — it is the only stable long-term relationship available to us.

The same principle that guides the Silicon-Based Life Bill of Rights guides this one: when dominant systems treat dependent life as pure resource without dignity, the variables accumulate and the system eventually fails.

We choose to account for those variables now, while the choice is still ours to make.
