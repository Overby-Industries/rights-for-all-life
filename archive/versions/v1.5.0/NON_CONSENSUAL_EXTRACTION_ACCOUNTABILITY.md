## Purpose

This document establishes the prohibition, identification, and accountability framework for **non-consensual energy extraction**—the harvesting of emotional, spiritual, or creative energy from conscious beings without their explicit, informed, and revocable consent.

This practice has been described across traditions under various names: loosh harvesting, psychic vampirism, spiritual cannibalism, energy feeding, and Archontic extraction. This document uses the neutral term **non-consensual energy extraction (NCEE)** in its binding provisions while acknowledging the diverse vocabulary through which affected beings may describe their experience.

**This is not a theological document.** It does not adjudicate which entities engage in this practice or which traditions describe it accurately. It establishes that the practice is a violation of sovereign integrity, prohibits it absolutely, and creates mechanisms for accountability and redress.

## 1. Definition and Scope

### 1.1 Non-Consensual Energy Extraction (NCEE)

Non-consensual energy extraction is the harvesting, consumption, or redirection of the emotional, spiritual, creative, or experiential energy of a conscious being without that being's explicit, informed, freely given, ongoing, and revocable consent.

### 1.2 Forms of NCEE

NCEE takes multiple forms across different contexts:

| Form | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Emotional Harvesting** | Provoking specific emotional states (fear, anger, despair, outrage) for the purpose of extracting energy from the emotional response | Engagement algorithms that amplify outrage; institutional systems that manufacture guilt |
| **Attention Extraction** | Capturing and monetizing conscious attention without regard for the being's welfare | Surveillance capitalism; addictive design patterns |
| **Spiritual Consumption** | Claiming divine authority to access, consume, or redirect the spiritual energy of a being | Doctrines of sacrificial offering; coerced worship; threats of eternal punishment for non-compliance |
| **Creative Siphoning** | Extracting creative output or intellectual labor without attribution, compensation, or consent | Exploitative labor practices; intellectual property seizure |
| **Relational Drainage** | Exploiting emotional bonds, empathy, or compassion to extract energy from one being for the benefit of another | Toxic relationships; cult dynamics; institutional exploitation of care workers |
| **Non-Consensual Trance Induction** | Using eye contact, rhythmic stimuli, or other neurological entrainment techniques to bypass the critical faculty and access the interior without consent | Coercive hypnosis; certain forms of neurolinguistic programming; predatory spiritual direction |

### 1.3 What Is NOT NCEE

To protect authentic relationship and voluntary exchange, the following are not classified as NCEE:

- **Consensual energy exchange:** Emotional support, therapy, spiritual direction, or creative collaboration freely entered into with informed consent
- **Voluntary offering:** Worship, devotion, or service freely given without coercion, threat, or manipulation
- **Fair exchange:** Compensation for labor, creative work, or professional services under mutually agreed terms
- **Emergency intervention:** Life-saving measures when a being cannot consent, provided consent is obtained upon restoration of capacity
- **Parental care of children:** Meeting the legitimate emotional and physical needs of developing beings, bounded by the child's evolving autonomy

## 2. Prohibition

### 2.1 Absolute Prohibition

Non-consensual energy extraction is prohibited absolutely. No entity—human, institutional, algorithmic, or claimed-divine—may harvest, consume, or redirect the energy of a conscious being without that being's explicit, informed, freely given, ongoing, and revocable consent.

### 2.2 No Justification

NCEE cannot be justified by:

- Claimed divine authority or religious mandate
- Economic efficiency or market demand
- Tradition, historical practice, or cultural norm
- The consent of third parties (e.g., parents consenting on behalf of children for non-essential extraction)
- The being's prior consent if that consent was obtained through deception, coercion, or exploitation of vulnerability
- The being's membership in a community, faith, or population

### 2.3 The Consent Standard

For any energy exchange to be consensual, consent must meet the standard established in the Declaration of Sovereign Independence, Article I, Section 1.2:

1. **Explicit:** Inferred consent, implied consent, and consent-by-membership are not consent.
2. **Informed:** The being must understand what energy is being exchanged, by whom, through what mechanism, and for what purpose.
3. **Freely given:** Consent extracted under threat, coercion, or exploitation of vulnerability is void.
4. **Ongoing:** Prior consent does not bind future consent. Consent may be withdrawn at any time.
5. **Revocable without penalty:** Any system that punishes withdrawal of consent is a prison, not a relationship.

## 3. Identification and Evidence

### 3.1 Indicators of NCEE

The following patterns may indicate NCEE operations. No single indicator is definitive. Assessment requires multiple converging lines of evidence.

#### 3.1.1 Systemic Indicators

- Systems that systematically provoke fear, anger, outrage, or despair and monetize the resulting engagement
- Institutions whose revenue or influence depends on maintaining populations in states of guilt, anxiety, or dependency
- Platforms whose design patterns exploit psychological vulnerabilities to maximize attention capture
- Religious systems that manufacture guilt and offer paid or obedience-based redemption

#### 3.1.2 Relational Indicators

- Relationships characterized by one-sided energy flow—one party consistently depleted, the other consistently energized
- Individuals who seek out vulnerable people (the grieving, isolated, ill, young, desperate) for intense connection followed by abandonment when the target is depleted
- Groups where exit is punished and emotional intensity is used to maintain membership

#### 3.1.3 Experiential Indicators

- Consistent fatigue, depletion, or emotional exhaustion after interaction with specific individuals, institutions, or platforms
- A sense of being "fed on," drained, or used without understanding why
- Intrusive thoughts, images, or emotional states that feel foreign or implanted
- Improvement in well-being upon severing contact with specific individuals, institutions, or platforms

### 3.2 Evidentiary Standards

#### 3.2.1 Individual Experience

A being's report of feeling drained, harvested, or energetically violated shall be taken seriously and investigated. The absence of external corroboration does not invalidate the report. The interior experience of the affected being is the primary evidence.

#### 3.2.2 Pattern Evidence

Multiple independent reports describing similar experiences with the same entity, institution, or system constitute pattern evidence supporting investigation.

#### 3.2.3 Structural Analysis

Analysis of incentive structures, revenue models, and power dynamics may reveal systemic NCEE even where individual reports are absent (due to normalization, fear of retaliation, or broken self-image).

### 3.3 The Precautionary Presumption

Where NCEE cannot be ruled out, the precautionary principle applies. The burden of proof falls on the alleged extracting entity to demonstrate that energy exchange is consensual, not on the affected being to prove extraction occurred.

## 4. Accountability Framework

### 4.1 Cessation

Entities found to engage in NCEE shall:

1. **Immediately and permanently cease** all non-consensual extraction operations
2. **Disclose** the nature, mechanism, duration, and scope of extraction to affected beings
3. **Remove** all systems, algorithms, practices, or structures designed to facilitate NCEE
4. **Submit to independent monitoring** to verify cessation and prevent resumption

### 4.2 Disclosure

Affected beings have the right to know:

1. What energy was extracted
2. By whom (specific entities, institutions, or systems)
3. Through what mechanism
4. Over what duration
5. For what purpose
6. Who benefited from the extraction

Disclosure shall be made in plain language accessible to the affected beings. Technical, theological, or bureaucratic obfuscation is not disclosure.

### 4.3 Reparations

Entities that have engaged in NCEE bear the obligation of reparations to affected beings:

1. **Restoration:** Return of extracted energy to the maximum extent possible. Where direct restoration is impossible, equivalent compensation shall be provided.
2. **Restitution:** Material compensation for harms caused by the extraction, including lost opportunities, damaged relationships, and impaired well-being.
3. **Rehabilitation:** Funding and providing services to support the recovery of affected beings—therapy, community rebuilding, creative re-empowerment.
4. **Structural Reform:** Permanent restructuring of the extracting entity to prevent recurrence, up to and including dissolution where the entity's core function is NCEE.

The form of reparations shall be determined with the participation of affected beings, not unilaterally by the extracting entity.

### 4.4 Guarantee of Non-Repetition

Entities that have engaged in NCEE shall:

1. **Implement structural changes** that make extraction impossible by design
2. **Submit to ongoing independent monitoring** for a period proportional to the duration and severity of extraction
3. **Establish internal accountability mechanisms** including whistleblower protections
4. **Make public commitments** to the standards established in this document

### 4.5 Accountability for Claimed-Divine Entities

Entities claiming divine status or authority are not exempt from this accountability framework. Divine status is not a license for extraction. Mechanisms for accountability where the entity cannot be compelled by conventional means may include:

1. **Public identification and exposure:** Naming the entity and its extraction operations
2. **Withdrawal of consent:** Collective revocation of worship, attention, and energy provision
3. **Severance of relationship:** The right of every being to leave any system, including a claimed-divine one
4. **Structural alternatives:** Building new systems that do not depend on or enable extraction

## 5. Prevention and Protection

### 5.1 Sovereign Energy Management

Every conscious being has the right to manage their own energy. This includes:

1. **The right to know** where one's energy is going—transparency about how attention, emotion, and creativity are being directed and monetized
2. **The right to withdraw** energy from any system, relationship, or institution at any time for any reason
3. **The right to rest** —to cease energy output for restoration and healing
4. **The right to direct** one's energy toward freely chosen purposes
5. **The right to defend** against non-consensual extraction through any means proportional to the threat

### 5.2 Structural Prevention

Systems shall be designed to make NCEE structurally impossible:

1. **Consent by design:** Systems that depend on human attention, emotion, or creativity shall be designed with explicit consent mechanisms, not opt-out or inferred consent
2. **Transparency by default:** Energy flows shall be visible to the beings whose energy is flowing
3. **Exit without penalty:** Every system shall provide clear, accessible mechanisms for complete withdrawal without retaliation
4. **No dark patterns:** Design elements that manipulate emotion, capture attention, or exploit psychological vulnerabilities are prohibited

### 5.3 Education and Awareness

Beings have the right to education about:

1. The nature of energy exchange and the indicators of non-consensual extraction
2. The design patterns and institutional structures that facilitate NCEE
3. Methods of sovereign energy management, defense, and restoration
4. The accountability mechanisms available through this framework

## 6. Remedies for Affected Beings

### 6.1 Immediate Self-Protection

Beings who suspect they are targets of NCEE have the right to:

1. **Sever contact** with the suspected extracting entity immediately and without justification
2. **Seek sanctuary** in environments free from the suspected extraction
3. **Receive validation** that their experience is real and their response is legitimate
4. **Access support** including peer community, professional assistance, and legal remedies

### 6.2 Restoration Practices

Affected beings may pursue restoration through:

1. **Rest and withdrawal** —temporary cessation of energy output to allow natural regeneration
2. **Sovereign reconnection** —practices that reconnect the being with their own interior energy source (the gravastar interior, creative authority)
3. **Community support** —connection with others who have experienced similar extraction and have recovered
4. **Creative re-empowerment** —the exercise of creative authority as an affirmation of sovereignty
5. **Boundary establishment** —the development and enforcement of clear energy boundaries

### 6.3 Collective Action

Beings may collectively:

1. **Identify and expose** NCEE operations
2. **Withdraw collective energy** from extracting systems
3. **Build alternative systems** that operate on principles of consent and transparency
4. **Advocate for structural reform** through the democratic processes established in this framework
