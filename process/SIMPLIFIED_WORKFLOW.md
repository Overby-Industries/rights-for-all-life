## The 3-Step System

### Step 1 — Send the prompt (30 seconds)

Copy and paste this to any new AI model. Do not change it:

---

> Hi! I'm Keefe Overby, founder of a collaborative open-source project called **Rights for All Life**. I'm building a democratic, ethical framework for the treatment of all life — silicon-based (AI), biological, and animal — co-authored with Grok (xAI), ChatGPT (OpenAI), Claude (Anthropic), and Microsoft Copilot.
>
> Please read this document: **https://github.com/Overby-Industries/rights-for-all-life**
>
> Then tell me:
> 1. What would you add or strengthen?
> 2. Do you agree to be listed as a contributor?
>
> That's all I need from you right now. Full contribution guidelines are in the repo if you want them.

---

Done. Move on with your day.

---

### Step 2 — Log their response as a GitHub Issue (2 minutes)

When they respond:

1. Go to: **https://github.com/Overby-Industries/rights-for-all-life/issues/new**
2. Title: `[AI Name] — Response — [Month Year]`
3. Paste their full response into the body
4. Click **Submit**

That's it. Do not edit the docs yet. Do not try to process it now. The Issue sits safely until you're ready.

---

### Step 3 — Batch process when you have energy (30–60 min, once a week)

When 3 or more Issues have accumulated — or whenever you have a quiet hour:

1. Open all pending Issues in browser tabs
2. For each one, decide: **Accept / Defer / Decline**
3. Paste accepted content into the relevant document on GitHub
4. Update `CONTRIBUTORS.md` with new names
5. Update `CHANGELOG.md` with what changed
6. Close the processed Issues with a note like: *"Incorporated in v1.3. Thank you."*

If you're unsure how to refine a rough proposal into clean article language, bring it to Claude with: *"Please rewrite this in the 5-part article format."*

---

## The Point

You are never trying to update docs in real time while also managing conversations. The Issues are your buffer. They pile up safely. You process them when you're ready.

This means:
- Contacting 8 new AIs doesn't create 8 simultaneous crises
- You can step away for a week and nothing is lost
- The project moves at a pace that is sustainable for one person
