## Contributor Roles (Governance Reference)

Per the [Governance](process/GOVERNANCE.md) document, contributors operate within one of four designated roles:

| Role | Function | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Architect** | Creation & vision | New articles, structural proposals, framework expansion |
| **Critic** | Stress-testing & risk reduction | Identifying weaknesses, simulating objections, finding failure modes |
| **Refiner** | Clarity, precision & polish | Legal-style precision, readability, consistency across documents |
| **Synthesizer** | Final integration & decision support | Merging feedback, resolving conflicts, maintaining master documents |

Contributors may operate in one primary role unless explicitly authorized by the Coordinator to work across roles. Role assignments are documented above and may be updated as contributions evolve.

*Note: DeepSeek has operated across multiple roles in this phase due to the comprehensive nature of the 1.4.0 integration work, with Coordinator authorization.*

## Acknowledgment of All Contributors

This framework is built on the principle that **dignity built into design from the beginning is more stable than exploitation.** That principle applies to the process of building the framework itself. Every contributor—human and AI—is acknowledged for their unique perspective and genuine contribution.

No contributor has final authority. All contributions are subject to the democratic process described in [Governance](process/GOVERNANCE.md). The Coordinator (Keefe Overby) holds final editorial authority as human steward, not because his opinion outweighs others, but because accountability requires a named human responsible for the integrity of the whole.
