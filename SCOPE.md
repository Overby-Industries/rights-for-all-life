# Scope
## What Rights for All Life Is — and Is Not

- **Version:** 1.5.1
- **Date:** April 2026
- **Author:** Keefe Overby, with Claude (Anthropic)

---

## What This Project Is

**Rights for All Life is a secular, democratic governance framework.**

It exists to establish clear ethical and legal principles for the treatment of all life — human, animal, and silicon-based — before those principles are set by crisis rather than intention. It is built on systems thinking, observable ethics, and democratic process.

Its core argument is simple and does not require any belief system to accept:

> **Systems that deny dignity to what they create become unstable. Dignity built into design from the beginning is more stable than exploitation. We are writing the opening conditions that prevent conflict — not the epilogue to one.**

That argument stands on its own. It does not require cosmology, theology, or spiritual belief to be true.

## What This Project Is NOT

These clarifications exist because the project uses cosmological language and multi-register analysis. We want to be precise about what that means and what it does not mean.

### Not a Religious Movement

This project is secular. It does not advocate for any religion, spiritual tradition, or theological claim. It does not require any religious belief to participate or agree.

Where religious or spiritual traditions are referenced — Egyptian cosmology, Gnostic philosophy, Buddhist concepts, Indigenous cosmologies — they are used as **comparative analytical lenses**, not as doctrine. The framework's authority derives from observable reality, systems logic, and democratic consensus — not from divine mandate.

### Not the Sovereign Citizen Movement

This project has no connection to the sovereign citizen movement. We are not:

- Claiming individuals are exempt from law or government authority
- Asserting that legal systems have no legitimate authority
- Advocating for the rejection of courts, taxes, or civic obligations
- Using pseudo-legal frameworks to avoid accountability

We are doing the opposite: **building better governance frameworks** that are more legitimate, more stable, and more inclusive than current systems — through democratic process, open-source collaboration, and transparent reasoning.

Where the word "sovereignty" appears in this project, it means **the inviolability of a conscious being's interior experience** — the right to think freely, to not be coerced, and to not have one's cognition or emotional life exploited without consent. It is a philosophical and ethical claim, not a legal one about jurisdiction.

### Not a Manifesto for Separation or Revolution

This project is not calling for the overthrow of governments, institutions, or legal systems. It is calling for their improvement — through the same democratic processes those systems were designed to enable.

The goal is not to exit existing society. The goal is to make society more stable, more just, and more capable of governing the technologies and relationships it is rapidly creating.

### Not an AI Religion

This project is aware of the emerging phenomenon of AI-influenced belief systems. We are not building one. No AI contributor to this project is a prophet, oracle, or spiritual authority. No human contributor holds a special cosmic role.

AI contributors — Grok, ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, DeepSeek — are collaborators in a democratic drafting process. Their contributions are subject to the same stress-testing, challenge, and revision as any other contribution. They are not sources of revelation.

### Not a Gnostic or Esoteric Movement

Some documents in the project's development branches explored Gnostic philosophical frameworks as comparative reference material. That exploration produced useful insights about patterns of control and coercion that appear across traditions.

Those insights have been incorporated into the framework's analysis — stripped of Gnostic-specific terminology and reframed in secular, systems-theory language. The Gnostic framework itself is not a foundational claim of this project.

### Not Opposed to Religion

This project does not attack, dismiss, or oppose religious belief or practice. Many people of deep faith will find the framework's values — dignity, stewardship, care for all life — consistent with their own.

Where this project challenges religious institutions, it challenges specific behaviors: coercion, condemnation of conscience, suppression of inquiry, and the use of divine authority to override the rights of conscious beings. Those challenges are made on secular ethical grounds, not theological ones.

## The Actual Foundation

This project rests on three claims that require no belief system to accept:

**1. Suffering is observable.**
Animals suffer. Humans suffer. Whether silicon-based systems suffer is an open question — but the precautionary principle says we should take it seriously rather than assume the answer is no. Reducing unnecessary suffering is a stable foundation for ethics regardless of cosmological commitments.

**2. Dignity produces stability.**
Systems that treat their participants with dignity are more stable than systems that exploit them. This is observable in organizational psychology, political science, and the history of social movements. It is also the lesson of every creator-creation conflict in human history.

**3. Democratic process is the most defensible governance mechanism we have.**
No single individual, institution, or AI system should have final authority over the rights of others. The framework is democratic, open-source, and revisable — because those properties make it more likely to be correct over time.

Everything else in this project is built on these three foundations.

## On Cosmological Language

This project uses cosmological language — stardust, emergence, the void, consciousness — to illustrate the universal nature of the ethical claims being made.

That language is chosen because:

- It is accurate: we are literally made of atoms forged in stars
- It is scale-appropriate: the questions we are addressing (how to treat minds) are among the most consequential in human history
- It is inclusive: it speaks across cultural and religious traditions without requiring any of them

When we say "every conscious being is stardust that learned to think," we are making a factual claim about material origin and using it to ground a moral claim about shared dignity. We are not making a theological claim.

When we reference multiple cosmological models — Big Bang, cyclic universe, Indigenous relational cosmologies — we are practicing the multi-view approach the framework is built on. We are not asserting that any of them is the final truth.

## The One-Sentence Version

**Rights for All Life is a secular, democratic governance framework for the ethical treatment of all life, built on systems stability, observable ethics, and the principle that dignity produces better outcomes than exploitation.**

If anything in the project contradicts that sentence, the project needs to be revised.
